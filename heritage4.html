Nahargarh Fort, perched on the rugged Aravalli hills overlooking Jaipur, is a majestic structure that once served as a key defense fortification for the city. Built in 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the founder of Jaipur, the fort was originally meant to strengthen the city's security alongside Amer and Jaigarh Forts. The name "Nahargarh," meaning "abode of tigers," is linked to local legends, including one about a spirit named Nahar Singh, believed to have haunted the site. The fort's architecture is a blend of Rajput and Mughal styles, featuring ornate halls, intricate frescoes, and stunning courtyards. Madhavendra Bhawan, a section of the fort built by Sawai Madho Singh, houses beautifully decorated suites for the royal family.
