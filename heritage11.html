City Palace in Jaipur, Rajasthan, is a magnificent royal residence that stands as a testament to the grandeur and cultural heritage of the Rajput dynasty. Built by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the founder of Jaipur, in the 18th century, the palace is an architectural marvel that blends Rajput, Mughal, and European styles. The palace complex is a harmonious collection of courtyards, gardens, temples, and opulent rooms, showcasing the splendor of royal life. The Chandra Mahal (Moon Palace), the most iconic part of the complex, is the royal residence and is adorned with intricate artwork, grand balconies, and beautiful frescoes. Visitors can explore Mubarak Mahal, Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, and the Sileh Khana, which houses the royal armory. The palace also houses the Museum of the City Palace, exhibiting valuable artifacts, costumes, and paintings from Jaipur's royal history.
